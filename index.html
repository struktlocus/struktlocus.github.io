PHILOSOPHICAL MANIFESTO

1. The Crisis of Natural Languages

Modern linguistics faces fundamental problems with natural languages:

  • Lexical ambiguity ‚Äî Words in natural languages exhibit polysemy, where a single word may carry dozens of meanings. For example, the English word "run" has 645 definitions in the Oxford English Dictionary.
  • Cultural dependency ‚Äî Idioms, connotations, and associations create barriers for precise meaning transfer between different cultures. Japanese "Âíå" (wa) or German "Weltschmerz" lack exact equivalents.
  • Syntactic redundancy ‚Äî Conjugations, irregular verbs, and exceptions complicate communication without adding semantic value. English has over 200 irregular verbs, while languages like Arabic have complex root-and-pattern systems.
  • Context dependence ‚Äî Meaning often depends on situation, intonation, and gestures, making automated language processing extremely challenging.

StruktLocus offers a radical solution ‚Äî complete separation of meaning from lexical form through structural determinism.

2. Principle of Topological Determinism

At its core, StruktLocus is built on a revolutionary concept: an element's meaning is 100% determined by its structural position, not its internal properties.

This is achieved through:

  • Fixed utterance structure ‚Äî Each structural quantum consists of strictly defined positions (Focus, Orientator, Dynama, etc.)
  • Positional semantics ‚Äî An element's meaning depends entirely on its slot position, not its form
  • Topological connectivity ‚Äî Relationships between elements are determined by their relative positions

3. Absolute Structural Universalism

StruktLocus implements maximum inclusivity:

  • Lexical agnosticism ‚Äî Any symbols (words, emoji, mathematical notations) can be used if they fit the slot's semantic class
  • Cultural neutrality ‚Äî No ties to specific cultural concepts
  • Multimodality ‚Äî Expressible through different modalities (text, graphics, sounds)

4. Cognitive Advantages

Psycholinguistic research demonstrates:

  • Structural languages activate parietal lobes responsible for spatial reasoning
  • Reduced cognitive load by eliminating lexical ambiguity
  • Faster acquisition compared to natural languages (artificial language experiments)
